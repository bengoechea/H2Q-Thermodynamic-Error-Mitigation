## Technical Profile

Kenneth A. Mendoza is an independent quantum computing researcher specializing in error mitigation methodologies. He is the inventor of the Hybrid Harmonic Quantum (H²Q) approach, a novel error mitigation technique that applies thermodynamic constraints to reduce quantum computational errors. His work is documented in US Provisional Patents 63/927,371 (November 2024) and 63/933,465 (December 2024).

## Current Research

**H²Q Error Mitigation Methodology**

The H²Q approach addresses fundamental challenges in QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm) by:

- Applying harmonic oscillator theory to constrain quantum state evolution
- Using thermodynamic principles to filter physically implausible solutions
- Implementing validation frameworks that distinguish genuine quantum advantage from classical simulation artifacts
- Achieving 79.7% reduction in false positives in MaxCut optimization benchmarks

**Technical Implementation**

- Built on Qiskit framework with custom error mitigation protocols
- Developed comparative analysis tools for quantum vs. classical optimization
- Created validation methodologies for quantum advantage tracking
- Implemented simulation frameworks for benchmarking error mitigation approaches
