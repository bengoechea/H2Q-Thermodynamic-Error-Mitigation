# Kenneth A. Mendoza - Complete Professional Biography

## Overview
Kenneth A. Mendoza is an independent quantum computing researcher and inventor with over 35 years of experience spanning molecular biology, hardware validation, proteomics research, and quantum computing innovation. He is the inventor of the Hybrid Harmonic Quantum (H²Q) error mitigation methodology, filed under US Provisional Patent 63/927,371 (November 2024) and 63/933,465 (December 2024).

## Current Focus
Developing and validating the H²Q error mitigation approach, which uses thermodynamic principles to reduce quantum computational errors. Recent simulations demonstrate a 79.7% reduction in false positives when applied to QAOA optimization problems.

Kenneth's 35-year career reflects a consistent pattern of tackling complex technical challenges across diverse domains. His current work on H²Q error mitigation represents a synthesis of insights from all these domains - applying thermodynamic principles from physics, validation methodologies from hardware testing, and analytical rigor from biological research to address fundamental challenges in quantum computing.

This interdisciplinary background enables Kenneth to approach quantum computing problems from unique angles, identifying opportunities that might not be apparent to researchers with more conventional backgrounds in the field.
